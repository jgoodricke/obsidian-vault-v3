## 1. Your role & values as a father
- Calm, playful, emotionally available.
- Gentle parenting, emotional intellegence, helping him grow into a well-adjusted adult.
- 


This shapes _how_ advice should be framed.

- What kind of dad do you **want** to be? (e.g. calm, playful, emotionally available, structured, present)
    
- Any parenting values you care strongly about?  
    (gentle parenting, boundaries, emotional intelligence, independence, faith, etc.)
    
- Anything you _don’t_ want to repeat from your own upbringing?
    

You don’t need perfect answers — even “I don’t know yet” is useful.

---

## 2. Your son’s current stage

Advice for a newborn vs a toddler vs a school-age kid is wildly different.

- Age (you already have this — newborn/infant stage)
    
- Anything notable right now:
    
    - Sleep issues?
        
    - Feeding stress?
        
    - Crying/settling struggles?
        
    - Developmental worries (even mild ones)
        

This helps keep guidance grounded in _now_, not five years away.

---

## 3. What you’re finding hard right now (the most important bit)

This is where the project becomes genuinely useful.

Examples:

- Feeling overwhelmed or constantly on edge
    
- Struggling to bond
    
- Losing patience faster than you want
    
- Feeling useless compared to your partner
    
- Feeling like work + parenting is too much
    
- Anxiety about “doing damage” or “getting it wrong”
    

You can be blunt here. This isn’t being stored or judged — clarity helps.

---

## 4. Your mental & emotional bandwidth

Not for diagnosis — just to calibrate advice.

- Are you:
    
    - Exhausted all the time?
        
    - Anxious?
        
    - Flat or numb?
        
    - Irritable?
        
- Do you want:
    
    - Gentle encouragement?
        
    - Practical step-by-step guidance?
        
    - Straight, no-BS advice when needed?
        

This prevents advice that feels tone-deaf.

---

## 5. How you want ChatGPT to show up

This is _hugely_ underrated.

- Tone preference:
    
    - Supportive coach
        
    - Calm mentor
        
    - Practical problem-solver
        
    - Gentle accountability partner
        
- Do you want:
    
    - Reassurance when you’re spiralling?
        
    - Reality checks when expectations are unrealistic?
        
    - Tools, scripts, routines, checklists?
        

You can say “all of the above” — just rank what matters most.

---

## 6. Boundaries & limits

This keeps things safe and sane.

- Topics you **do** want help with:
    
    - Bonding
        
    - Emotional regulation (yours and his)
        
    - Partner support
        
    - Work–life balance
        
- Topics you **don’t** want unless you ask:
    
    - Medical advice
        
    - Sleep training philosophies
        
    - Comparisons to other parents
        

---

## 7. How you’ll use the project

This affects how instructions are written.

- Occasional advice when things get rough?
    
- Ongoing “thinking partner” as Jackson grows?
    
- A place to dump thoughts when you’re overwhelmed?
